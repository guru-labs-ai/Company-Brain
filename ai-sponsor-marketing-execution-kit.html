1 · Meta ads — what to actually run

Settings, and why each one

Objective: registration / lead, not purchase. This is the single most important setting. At a $5 price point we'd never generate enough purchases a week for Meta's algorithm to learn from, so a purchase-optimised campaign burns budget hunting a signal that never arrives. Optimise for the event we can actually produce.

Targeting: broad. Meta's delivery now reads the creative and the primary text to decide who sees it, rather than leaning on stacked interests. That means the recovery vocabulary in the copy is the targeting — which is why the texts below deliberately use the language of the rooms.

Placements: automatic, but review. Adjacency matters more than usual for this audience.

One angle per ad set, so we learn which angle pulls rather than which combination did.

Before any of this copy runs

All three texts below lead on WhatsApp, because it's our sharpest genuine difference. They cannot run until WhatsApp is live. If we have to launch first, the web-chat versions lose the strongest line we have — worth waiting rather than spending on the weaker product.

Recovery is a Meta sensitive category. The rule every line here follows: describe the help, never label the reader. "Struggling with addiction?" gets rejected. Longer copy means more surface area to trip it, so each one needs a compliance read before it goes in.

META · ANo app · lives in WhatsApp
Highlighted = the first ~125 characters, before "See more"
There's no app to download. AI Sponsor lives inside WhatsApp — already open on your phone, already there at 2am.

That's the whole idea. Not another login, not another icon on a home screen you'll delete in a week. Just a message thread, in the place you already message from.

It's built on the 12 steps, and it knows them properly — AA, NA, Al-Anon, ACA, GA, OA, SLAA. It knows what a home group is. It knows what Step 4 asks of you and why people stall on Step 9. It knows what HALT means at 11pm.

It remembers you, too. Your clean date. What you're working through. What you said last week and how it turned out. You don't start over every time you open it.

What it isn't: a replacement for your sponsor, or for the rooms, or for a therapist. Nothing replaces someone who has walked it. It's for the hours in between — after the meeting ends, before your sponsor is awake, when you don't want to be the person calling at 3am again.

Completely private. Nothing you say is shared with anyone.

And every membership pays for someone who can't afford one.

Free for 30 days. $5 a month after that.
Why this one first: it leads on the only thing no competitor can copy quickly — every one of them is an app-store download. The "what it isn't" paragraph is deliberate: it's honest, it pre-empts the objection this audience always raises, and it's the same position our content takes.
META · BThe hours in between · 3am
Highlighted = the first ~125 characters, before "See more"
Recovery doesn't keep office hours. The hard part usually lands late — after the meeting, when everyone's asleep.

That's the gap. Meetings end. Sponsors have jobs and families and their own recovery. And the moment that matters most is rarely at a convenient hour.

AI Sponsor is there for exactly that gap. It's in WhatsApp, so there's nothing to download and nothing to open — you just message, the way you'd message anyone. It answers in seconds, at any hour, and it doesn't sigh.

It's built on the 12 steps and it knows your programme, whichever one you're in. It remembers what you're carrying, so you're not explaining yourself from scratch at midnight.

It won't replace your sponsor and it isn't trying to. It's what you have at 3am so you don't have to white-knuckle it alone until morning.

Private. Anonymous. Free for 30 days.

Every membership gives one away to someone who can't afford it.
Test this against A. A sells the difference; B sells the feeling. Running them head to head tells us whether differentiation or emotion moves this audience before we scale anything.
META · CEvery programme · breadth
Highlighted = the first ~125 characters, before "See more"
AA. NA. Al-Anon. ACA. GA. OA. Whatever room you sit in, AI Sponsor already speaks your fellowship's language.

Most recovery apps are built for one thing — usually drinking. But the steps run through a lot more than that, and the person going to Al-Anon because of someone else's drinking needs support just as much as the person counting days.

AI Sponsor knows the differences. It knows Al-Anon isn't AA. It knows what a qualifier is, what a pigeon is, what "keep coming back" actually means. It knows the literature and it won't mix up the traditions.

It lives in WhatsApp, so there's no app to download. It remembers your story between conversations. And it's there at whatever hour the difficult part shows up.

Not a replacement for a sponsor or a meeting — something for the space between them.

Private, anonymous, free for 30 days. Every membership funds a free one for someone who can't afford it.
Strategic value beyond the ad: this angle covers the family and behavioural programmes, which are the ones Google's ad restriction explicitly does not cover. It's the one message that can run on both platforms.

Headlines and creative

Headlines to pair with any of the above: "Always here. Day or night." · "Your recovery, in your messages." · "The hours between meetings." · "No app. Just a message."

Creative: no AI-generated video. It doesn't perform in this category and it reads as hollow to an audience that is unusually good at spotting insincerity. Real UGC-style footage or plain, quiet typographic stills. Nothing that looks like stock recovery imagery — no sunrises, no silhouettes on beaches.

2 · Google ads — what to actually run

Google restricts this category — so we only run what's outside it

Their policy names "mutual help organizations." The behavioural and family programmes are explicitly out of scope, which is the only clean route in. Both campaigns below are built to test that, not to chase volume.

Landing pages first. Google reviews the destination, not just the keyword. Sending Debtors Anonymous ads to our AA/NA-led homepage could get the whole account classified as addiction services — killing the exact thing we're testing.

Campaign 1 — Debtors Anonymous (the policy probe)

Judge this on approval, not clicks. DA is a small fellowship and volume will be thin. If these ads clear review, the behavioural carve-out is proven and Al-Anon, GA, SLAA and OA all open behind it. That's the deliverable.

3 · getaisponsor.com — what to add

The actual problem with the site

It has two indexable pages. Homepage and registration — that's the entire sitemap. The AEO groundwork from June is all in place (JSON-LD, FAQ schema, llms.txt, robots, Search Console verified), but it's sitting on a two-page site. There's almost nothing for Google or an AI engine to read.

Structure: add /guides/, one page each. Article + FAQPage schema on every one, linked from the homepage footer and from each other, and added to the sitemap.

The format rule that matters: open every page with a self-contained answer block of roughly 150 words that fully answers the page's question on its own. That block is what AI engines lift and cite. Question-based headings below it. This comes straight from the GEO audit criteria in the Claude SEO tooling Matt sent.

The content rule: category and culture, never clinical advice. No detox guidance, no "signs of alcoholism," no relapse-prevention claims. We'd lose to NIH and Mayo Clinic, and Google puts a crisis hotline above those queries for good reason.

/guides/what-is-an-ai-sponsor
What is an AI sponsor?
Targets: "ai sponsor", "what is an ai sponsor", "ai sobriety sponsor"
Answer block: a plain definition — a 24/7 conversational companion built on the 12 steps that you message like a person, which remembers your story between conversations. What it does, what it costs, what it explicitly is not. No marketing language in the block itself; it has to read like a reference answer, because that's what gets quoted.
Why it's ours to win: we're the category, and the current results are App Store listings and a Reddit thread.
/guides/can-ai-replace-your-sponsor
Can an AI replace your AA sponsor?
Targets: "can ai be my aa sponsor" — a live "People also ask" question on Google right now.
Answer block: no — and then exactly why. An AI can't work the steps with you, can't share its own experience, can't meet you for coffee, can't notice you've gone quiet in a room. Then what it genuinely does cover: the hours between, the 3am gap, the weeks you're still looking for a sponsor.
Why this one matters most: publishing the honest answer, as the company selling the thing, is the most trust-building page we could put up — and it's the reason an AI engine would cite us rather than a landing page. Marketing copy doesn't get quoted; straight answers do.
/guides/is-it-anonymous
Is talking to an AI sponsor actually private?
Targets: privacy and anonymity queries — the single biggest objection in this audience.
Answer block: what's stored, what isn't, who can see it, what anonymity means here versus what it means in the rooms. Concrete and checkable, not reassuring noise.
Note: this must match what the product actually does. It's also the page that will get scrutinised hardest, so it needs the deletion path finished behind it.
/guides/support-for-aa-na-al-anon
AI recovery support for AA, NA, Al-Anon and the other programmes
Targets: the long tail — one section per fellowship, each answering "does it know my programme."
Answer block: the breadth claim, made specific. What differs between fellowships and how the sponsor handles each.
Double duty: the Al-Anon and behavioural sections are the audience Google's ad restriction doesn't cover — so this is the one page where organic and paid can both point at the same people.
/guides/sobriety-chips-and-medallions
Sobriety chips and medallions: what each one means
Targets: "sobriety chips", "aa medallions", "1 year sobriety chip" — commercial intent, real volume.
Answer block: the milestones and what each marks — 24 hours, 30 / 60 / 90 days, 6 and 9 months, then a medallion at a year and every year after. Colours, who hands them over, why people keep them for decades.
The specific opportunity: Google is already generating an AI Overview on this query, currently citing a merchant's shop page. That's a citation slot with no strong owner. Close honestly on what we actually do today — your sponsor knows what day you're on and marks it with you — without pretending we sell anything.
Bonus: it's the same milestone list the digital token would render, so the page and the feature are one piece of work.
